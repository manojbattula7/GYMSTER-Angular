CLASSES SHEDULE

WORKING HOURS

6.00AM - 8.00AM

POWER LIFTING

JHON DEO
Focus on Proper Technique: Emphasize correct form and technique before adding heavy weights. Proper execution reduces the risk of injury and maximizes results.
Gradual Weight Progression: Increase weights progressively to challenge yourself without compromising form. Incremental progress leads to long-term strength gains.
Prioritize Safety and Hydration: Stay hydrated and take adequate rest between sets. Listen to your body and stop if you feel discomfort. Safety comes first.
Engage in Warm-Up and Cool-Down: Begin with a dynamic warm-up to prepare muscles and joints. End with static stretches to improve flexibility and aid recovery.
Celebrate Achievements: Acknowledge your progress, whether it's lifting heavier weights or mastering better technique. Positive milestones drive motivation.

8.00AM - 10.00AM

BODY BUILDING

JAMES TAYLOR
Proper Form and Range of Motion: Prioritize correct exercise form over lifting heavy weights. Focus on controlled movements and a full range of motion to target muscles effectively and prevent injuries.
Structured Workout Plan: Follow a structured routine that targets different muscle groups on different days. This approach allows for balanced muscle development and prevents overtraining.
Vary Intensity and Reps: Alternate between different rep ranges (e.g., heavy weights with lower reps, moderate weights with moderate reps) to stimulate muscle growth through diverse stimuli.
Nutrition and Hydration: Fuel your body with proper nutrition and stay hydrated throughout the workout. Adequate protein intake supports muscle repair and growth.
Progressive Overload: Continuously challenge your muscles by gradually increasing weights or resistance. Progressive overload is key to building muscle strength and size over time.

10.00AM - 12.00PM

CARDIO PROGRAM

JACK SPARROW
Dynamic Warm-Up: Begin with an energetic warm-up that includes dynamic stretches and light cardio to prepare your muscles and raise your heart rate.
Maintain Target Heart Rate: Aim for a sustained elevated heart rate within your target zone during the cardio session. This helps maximize cardiovascular benefits.
Vary Intensity Levels: Incorporate interval training by alternating between high-intensity bursts and lower-intensity periods. This approach improves endurance and burns more calories.
Stay Hydrated: Keep a water bottle nearby and take sips during breaks to stay hydrated throughout the session.
Cool Down and Stretch: End the class with a gradual cool-down, followed by static stretches to help reduce muscle tension and promote flexibility.

12.00PM - 2.00PM

WEIGHT LOOSE

ROBERT SMITH
Balanced Nutrition: Focus on a well-rounded diet that includes lean proteins, whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats. Proper nutrition is crucial for effective weight loss.
Regular Exercise Routine: Commit to a consistent exercise routine that combines cardiovascular workouts, strength training, and flexibility exercises to support weight loss.
Portion Control: Pay attention to portion sizes to avoid overeating. Smaller, balanced meals throughout the day can help manage calorie intake.
Hydration and Mindful Eating: Stay hydrated and practice mindful eating by savoring each bite. Drinking water before meals can help control appetite.
Track Progress and Set Goals: Keep a journal to track your food intake, exercise, and progress. Set achievable short-term and long-term goals to stay motivated.

2.00PM - 4.00PM

FITNESS PROGRAM

ADAM PHILLIPS
Full-Body Workout: Engage in exercises that target various muscle groups for a comprehensive fitness routine. Incorporate cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises.
Proper Warm-Up and Cool-Down: Begin with a dynamic warm-up to prepare muscles and joints. End with static stretches to promote flexibility and recovery.
Functional Movements: Include exercises that mimic real-life movements to improve overall functional fitness. This can enhance daily activities and reduce the risk of injury.
Variation and Progression: Continuously challenge yourself by varying workout routines and increasing intensity or resistance over time. This prevents plateaus and supports continuous improvement.
Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to how your body responds to different exercises. If something causes discomfort or pain, modify or skip it, and consult a trainer if needed.

4.00PM - 6.00PM

CROSSFIT CLASS

JAMES ALIEN
Functional Movements: Focus on compound movements that engage multiple muscle groups, such as squats, deadlifts, and overhead presses. These movements mimic real-life activities and promote overall strength.
Proper Technique: Prioritize correct form and technique over speed or heavy weights. Proper execution is crucial to prevent injuries and optimize results.
Warming Up: Begin with a dynamic warm-up that includes mobility exercises and light cardio to prepare your body for the intensity of the CrossFit workout.
Scaling and Modification: Adjust exercises and weights to match your fitness level. CrossFit encourages scalability, allowing participants of different abilities to work out together.
Community Support: Embrace the camaraderie and community aspect of CrossFit. Encourage and support your fellow participants as you all work toward your fitness goals.

6.00PM - 8.00PM

MUSCLE BUILDING

PETTER JOHN
Structured Routine: Follow a well-structured workout routine that targets different muscle groups on different days. This allows for adequate recovery and balanced muscle development.
Compound Exercises: Include compound movements such as squats, deadlifts, bench presses, and rows. These exercises engage multiple muscle groups, promoting overall muscle growth.
Progressive Overload: Continuously challenge your muscles by gradually increasing weights or resistance. Progressive overload is key to building muscle strength and size.
Nutrition and Recovery: Prioritize protein-rich meals to support muscle repair and growth. Get adequate rest and sleep for optimal recovery.
Form and Control: Focus on controlled repetitions with proper form. Avoid using momentum or swinging to lift weights, as this can compromise results and increase the risk of injury.

8.00PM - 10.00PM

YOGA CLASS

JESSY REO
Mindful Breathing: Focus on controlled breathing throughout the class. Deep and mindful breathing helps calm the mind and enhances the yoga practice.
Proper Alignment: Pay attention to proper alignment in each yoga pose. Alignment ensures safety, prevents strain, and maximizes the benefits of the practice.
Gentle Warm-Up: Begin with gentle stretches to warm up the body and prepare for deeper poses. Warming up helps reduce the risk of injury.
Mind-Body Connection: Stay present and mindful during the practice. Connect with your body, thoughts, and emotions to experience the full benefits of yoga.
Cool-Down and Relaxation: End the class with relaxing poses and a guided relaxation (savasana). This helps reduce stress, promote relaxation, and integrate the benefits of the practice.
